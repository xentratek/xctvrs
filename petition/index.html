Petition

Support Fair and Accountable VRS Access

This petition page is focused on fair access, service reliability, public accountability, and meaningful improvement in Video Relay Service systems. The goal is to create a clear public statement that supporters can understand, share, and strengthen.

Read Statement Support This Petition
Access Communication access must be dependable, not inconsistent.

Visibility The real impact on users should not stay hidden or minimized.

Formal Statement

We call for fair, reliable, and respectful access to Video Relay Service for the people who depend on it in everyday life. When communication access becomes unstable, delayed, poorly supported, or difficult to trust, the impact goes far beyond inconvenience. It affects safety, independence, employment, family connection, and equal participation.

This petition exists to document those concerns clearly, raise public awareness, and support practical improvements that can be measured over time. The request is simple: improve service reliability, strengthen accountability, and treat communication access as essential.

We believe the people who rely on VRS deserve a system that reflects consistency, transparency, and respect. This page is a starting point for building that record.

What this petition is asking for

  • More dependable VRS service access
  • Clearer accountability when service issues occur
  • Better visibility into the impact on users
  • Stronger response standards and follow-up
  • A more respectful and practical advocacy record

Why this matters

  • Communication access affects real daily life
  • Unresolved service gaps can create wider harm
  • Public awareness is needed beyond private complaints
  • Consistent documentation helps support real change

Suggested campaign roadmap

Phase 1 — Define the case
Finalize the statement, summarize the problem, and identify the requested improvements.
Phase 2 — Gather support
Collect supporter names, short accounts, and evidence that shows the concern clearly.
Phase 3 — Public outreach
Share the petition page and make the issue easier for others to understand.
Phase 4 — Escalate with evidence
Package the statement, examples, and timeline into a stronger advocacy record.
